Hamster Hideout Archive

I am thinking of collecting from hamsterhideout.com, a now defunct hamster care website.
I used to frequent this website as a child who was interested in owning a hamster.
Back in the 2000's, pet websites such as these were the most reliable source of information for small animals, especially if you did not have access to an exotic vet. Pet stores were (and still are) notorious for giving out improper pet care sheets and sold products that were not suited for caging hamsters. Hamster Hideout provided information collated by vets, breeders, and longtime hamster owners that is easily availiable to anyone. They also hosted a forum where anyone with an account can ask questions, post pictures of their hammies, and discuss hamster care, as information on proper hamster care was constantly changing with new studies and information.

I also want to collect from Hamster Hideout to compare and contrast to how modern pet communities congregate today. These days, they are hosted on either Reddit or Discord. While Reddit has mostly kept its roots to the web of the 2000's, The way that communities are hosted limits access to information to the public. You need an account to even see information and it's not as easy to search for topics or questions that have already been discussed due to its chatroom format.

What's Missing/Lost on Hamster Hideout

  • Avatars
  • Signatures
  • Unpinned Forum Posts
  • Photos

What material to prioritize preserving?

I would prioritize preserving unpinned forum posts and photos posted in those posts. Some questions posed on the forum may not be covered in the pinned posts of the forum, and provides alternative perspectives on different topics. Photos provided in those forum threads can give us a glance at the technology used during the time of posting, and evolution of pet care of an individual throughout the course of their time in the forums.
